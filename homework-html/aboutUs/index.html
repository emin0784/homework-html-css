Bis Bez

Macedonian rock group created in 1964.in Skopje, under the strong influence of the legendary Beatles. It was formed after the breakup of the rock bands Biseri and Bezimeni. Stanislav Dlaka (guitar) and Lambe Smilevski (bass guitar) came to the band from the Biseri group, and from the Bezimeni group: Konstantin Ikonomov – Koce (guitar and keyboards), Dimitar Chemkov – Tsime (guitar), Dimitar Popankov (drums). and vocalist Stevce Janevski - Chivchi. This is the first line-up of the group, but later, other musicians joined the band.

Creation and Beginnings (1964-1968)

The band was formed in 1964. After the formation, in 1964 and 1965, Bis Bez played in local clubs and halls. In 1966, they started writing their first songs. Some of the songs they wrote were covers of well-known songs, but in the Macedonian language.

Fame and First Interruption (1968-1973)

They played several genres of rock music, such as hard rock, rock and roll, blues rock, psychedelic rock and pop rock. In April 1968 Bis Bez participated in the Yugoslav pop rock festival in Zagreb, playing the song "More falcon drinks". In 1970 won the "Zagreb Pop Festival" with their biggest hit "Should I Cry or Sing". Bis Bez played and became popular from 1964 to 1973. They stopped playing in 1973 when, as the band members say, " they swam into matrimonial waters."
